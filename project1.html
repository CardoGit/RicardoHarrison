Solar-Powered Water Purifier

Sustainability Renewable Energy Product Design CAD

Project Date: January 2022 - June 2022

Project Overview

A low-cost, portable water purification system designed for rural communities without access to clean water. The system uses solar thermal energy to purify water through distillation, eliminating 99.9% of pathogens and heavy metals.

Technical Specifications

Dimensions:
60cm × 45cm × 30cm
Weight:
8.5 kg
Daily Output:
15 liters (sunny conditions)
Materials:
Stainless steel, tempered glass, food-grade silicone

My Role

As the lead designer, I was responsible for the thermal system design, CAD modeling, prototype testing, and field trials. I collaborated with a team of 3 engineers to optimize the design for manufacturability and cost-effectiveness.

Key Features

  • Self-cleaning condensation surface
  • Modular design for easy repair
  • Integrated solar tracking (manual adjustment)
  • No electrical components - purely mechanical

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Low Efficiency in Cloudy Conditions

Initial prototypes showed significant drops in output during cloudy weather. We solved this by adding insulated thermal mass (water-filled chambers) that store heat during sunny periods and continue the distillation process when sunlight is reduced.

Challenge: Mineral Buildup

Hard water sources caused scaling that reduced efficiency. The solution was a removable, cleanable collector plate with a non-stick coating that could be easily maintained by end-users.
